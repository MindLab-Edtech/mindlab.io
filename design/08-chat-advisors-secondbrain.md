## 0. The frame (LOCKED by founder, 2026-07-08)

We are **not** positioning against ChatGPT. We are **extending the thinking process** — providing an **"another brain" / second-brain** capability on top of the system of record. The analyst keeps the judgment; the system extends their **range, recall, and the number of angles** they can hold at once.

> Founder's words: *"I don't think we are against ChatGPT — we are extending the 'thinking process' and provide another-brain capabilities for the work."*

**Core thesis (from the AVV reference, see §3):** *"The story isn't that AI replaces thinking: it's that AI hands time back to it."* Humans keep the "qualitative gut factors, X-factors"; the agent covers the busywork, the recall, and the range.

### Reconciling with the brand's "not a chatbot" language
The brand docs say *"deal-shaped, not chat-shaped,"* *"ChatGPT answers die in a chat history."* That is **not in conflict** with this section — it's the same differentiator (**grounded + remembered**) stated two ways:
- Old/defensive framing: "not a chatbot, ChatGPT forgets."
- **This section's framing (additive, confident):** "a second brain on your record that explores, verifies, and remembers."

Same truth, positive voice. This section is the *confident, additive* answer to "why not just ChatGPT" — it doesn't need to name the enemy. **Do not reintroduce an anti-ChatGPT lead.**

## 1. The three pillars

### 1a. Ask — the exploration & verification engine on the system of record
A connected chat/ask surface **on top of** the MindLab record. Not "ask AI to build a report." It's **exploration / data-mining / deep-brainstorming** for the questions that **don't fit a template field**.

The report/dashboard templates answer the *known, framework-shaped* questions. **Ask answers the ones you didn't know to put on the template** — the concerns that surface mid-diligence.

What it's for (all grounded in real product surfaces):
- **Cross-asset recall you can't hold in your head.** "Have we seen a cold-chain logistics deal before? How did it perform?" It mines across every deal + portfolio company. A human can't remember the whole book; this can.
- **Calculation & thesis verification.** Recompute owner earnings from raw financials; stress-test a claim; check a number. (The Buffett capture literally recomputes and says *"do NOT trust adjusted EBITDA."*)
- **Compare a live deal to an invested one.** Benchmark a new opportunity against a portfolio company you already own.
- **Align a deal to firm standards.** Check a target against your firm's investment criteria / approved knowledge / output standards (see `Firm-knowledge-scoring&output-standards.png`).

**Proof capture:** `chat-warren-buffet-moat.png` — the agent narrates its work ("I'll pull the live record for PraxiaFlow Health… the scout surfaced strong moat-relevant evidence… let me pull the two files that speak to switching costs"), then returns a structured **Moat Check** with a **verdict table**, a **break-the-moat scenario**, and a **kill question** — **every claim cited** to a source file (`[Management Call Transcript]`, `[Pitch Deck, Slide 10]`, `[Diligence Question List]`), with a source footer listing everything attached to the deal, and it flags itself as synthetic demo data. Composer shows **voice mode + code interpreter**. Tool/skill trace: `get_skill_instructions`, `search_entities`.

**AVV parallel:** their specialist agent "Ava" that "reliably pulls from our systems of record" (Carta, Vestberry, Drive, Attio). Ask is the record-grounded specialist, not a blank web chat.

### 1b. Advisors — frameworks of thinking, and you can add your own
A deal can be seen from many aspects; **one analyst can't cover them all at once.** Advisors are **famous thinking frameworks made queryable** — each a lens that gives you an angle you'd otherwise miss, and (via Ask) a way to **verify your assumptions and thesis against that framework.**

**Two tiers:**
1. **The canon (out of the box).** 15 named investor/operator lenses, grouped by discipline:
   - **Strategy** — Bezos, Buffett, Hastings
   - **Operations** — Barra, Cook, Grove
   - **Innovation** — Huang, Jobs, Musk
   - **Culture** — Brown, Nadella, Sinek
   - **Finance** — Dalio, Dimon, Munger
2. **Your own (the moat).** Advisors are **open/extensible.** Bring a framework, a book, a theory, your firm's own diligence playbook → MindLab builds you a **dedicated advisor, reusable across every deal.** Your proprietary process becomes a repeatable lens. *(This is the "tuned advisors" compounding moat from the internal brief, made concrete.)*

**The killer mechanic — suggested questions.** Each advisor arrives pre-loaded with the 3 sharp questions *they'd* ask. Buffett: *"What's the moat? Name the specific source(s)"* / *"What are the owner earnings? Recompute — do NOT trust adjusted EBITDA."* Munger: *"What's the path to failure? Invert."* **You don't have to think like Buffett — the board hands you Buffett's questions.** Each advisor also carries `skills on this portfolio` (`moat-check`, `compute-intrinsic-value`, `margin-of-safety-calc`) — they run real analyses, not flavor.

**Governance (from internal brief):** internal vs external advisor separation; external advisors answer **only from approved knowledge.**

**Proof capture:** `portfolio-advisors.png` — the FreshRoute AI opportunity's **Board of Advisors** tab: *"15 advisors · grounded in this opportunity's live record,"* one "Ask any advisor…" bar, the discipline-grouped grid, and a floating **"Ask PE Analyst"** generalist.

**AVV parallel:** their generalist ("Khoa") + specialist ("Ava") split — advisors are specialists tuned to a lens; the PE Analyst is the generalist.

### 1c. The pocket colleague — WhatsApp / Telegram (convenience, in the wild)
**⚠️ Founder-asserted; no capture; confirm live-vs-roadmap before writing hard copy.**

The same brain, **reachable when you're outside**, acting like an assistant/colleague you text. It **seeks information** and **does actions** (e.g. spin up a new opportunity). It's the thinnest possible entry point to the loop.

Scenarios (founder's):
- You meet a partner at a private party and want to **introduce them to something** on the spot.
- You get a lead over **coffee with a friend** — text your assistant *"start an opportunity on X,"* it creates the shell; you **follow up in the system later.**

Behavior: **capture in the wild → the record picks it up → you finish the diligence at your desk.** *"Start small — tell your assistant to set it up, follow it later."* This is the thinnest on-ramp to the `the-loop.html` "start thin, compound it" loop.

**AVV parallel:** their **Slack** integration = the always-available, visible-to-the-team colleague you can hand the next turn to. WhatsApp/Telegram is the *in-the-wild* version of that.

### The arc (why the three belong together)
1. **Ask** = range + recall you can't hold.
2. **Advisors** = angles one analyst can't cover alone (+ your own frameworks, reusable).
3. **Pocket colleague** = reach that captures opportunity anywhere and acts for you.

All three = **another brain / the analyst you never hired** — it hands the busywork, recall, and range back so you spend your time on judgment. Human approval still gates everything (**"AI prepares. Humans approve. MindLab remembers."**).
