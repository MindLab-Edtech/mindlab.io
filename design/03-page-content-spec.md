# 03 · Page Content Spec

Eleven sections, one argument. Copy below is **approved draft** — design with it as-is;
typographic needs may motivate small edits (flag them, don't silently make them).

Read-depth architecture the layout must serve:
- **10-second read** (partner, phone): S1 + S2 must work standalone.
- **3-minute read** (VP): + S4, S5, S6, S9.
- **15-minute read** (champion + compliance): everything, incl. FAQ.

Rule: every section header carries its conclusion — the H2s alone must sell (spine at the end of
this file). One idea per viewport. CTA appears at S1, after S7, and S11 — same verb every time.

---

## S1 · Hero

- **Eyebrow:** `FOR PRIVATE EQUITY DEAL TEAMS`
- **H1:** AI prepares. Humans approve. MindLab remembers.
- **Subhead:** MindLab turns CIMs, data rooms, spreadsheets, and portfolio updates into
  source-backed research your team reviews, approves, and reuses — so the next deal starts from
  everything the firm already knows.
- **Primary CTA:** `Bring one live deal →`
- **Secondary CTA:** `See the workflow`
- **Visual:** the hero product composition (spec: `04` §6.1) — a dense, credible product
  vignette showing a review queue, a cited claim, and an Approve action. NOT a gradient, NOT a
  chat bubble, NOT an abstract 3D shape.
- Nav (minimal): logo mark + wordmark · How it works · Security · FAQ · `Bring one live deal`.

*Design intent:* the mantra's three short sentences are a typographic gift — consider setting
them as three stacked lines with the middle line ("Humans approve.") carrying the accent color,
since the approval gate is the differentiator.

## S2 · The re-verification tax (stat bar)

- **Stat, set large:** 59% of finance professionals still manually re-verify or re-do AI outputs.
- **Kicker line:** That's not an AI problem. It's a workflow problem.
- **Source line, small but legible (not hidden):** Hebbia "Diligence Paradox" survey of 510 US
  finance professionals, Apr–May 2026.

*Design intent:* this replaces the customer-logo strip the company doesn't have yet. A verified
stat from a *competitor's own commissioned survey* does the credibility work. Treat
typographically like a pull-quote in a serious publication.

## S3 · The problem, told as three scenes

- **H2:** You don't have a data shortage. You have a memory problem.
- **Scene 1 — The third first-look.** The target you screened in 2024 is back with a new banker.
  Nobody can find the old work. An associate rebuilds it from scratch.
- **Scene 2 — The update that vanished.** The portco flagged margin pressure in March. It
  resurfaced in September — in a partner's memory, not in the record.
- **Scene 3 — The unanswerable question.** IC asks: "where's that churn number from?" The answer
  is a 17-tab spreadsheet and an analyst who left in June.
- **Coda line:** Your analysts are not slow. The workflow is slow.

*Design intent:* three narrow text columns or sequential editorial blocks. NO icon-and-blurb
cards. These read like war stories; let the type do the work. Each scene quietly maps to a
section below (S7 records, S6 monitoring, S4 provenance).

## S4 · The mechanism — R3 (signature section)

- **H2:** Nothing becomes a record until a human approves it.
- **Body (short):** MindLab runs on one pipeline: Resources → Reports → Records. AI drafts
  reports from your sources. Your team reviews, edits, and approves. Only approved work updates
  the firm's records.
- **Visual:** the R3 gate diagram (spec: `04` §6.2) — the page's centerpiece.
- **Three captions under/inside the diagram:**
  1. AI never silently writes to your records.
  2. Every claim carries its source.
  3. Every approval is logged.

## S5 · Workflow one — Target Brief

- **Label:** `WORKFLOW 01`
- **H2:** From data room to IC-ready first pass.
- **Body:** Point MindLab at a name, a website, or a data room. It returns a structured screening
  brief in your firm's memo format: business snapshot, market context, competitors, risk flags,
  and the diligence questions worth asking — every claim cited, and gaps flagged as gaps.
- **Visual:** Target Brief product vignette (content vocabulary in `06`).
- **Micro-line:** Where the blank page used to be.

## S6 · Workflow two — Portfolio Pulse

- **Label:** `WORKFLOW 02`
- **H2:** Know what changed before the Monday meeting.
- **Body:** MindLab watches your portfolio's updates, documents, and external signals, and drafts
  recurring what-changed briefs: covenant flags, narrative shifts, watch items. Each lands in the
  review queue; nothing enters the record until someone signs it.
- **Visual:** portfolio board vignette with two flagged companies (sample data in `06`).

## S7 · The compounding part — firm memory

- **H2:** Every deal makes the next one faster.
- **Body:** Approved briefs, memos, and updates become Company Records — versioned, sourced, and
  searchable. When a target comes back two years later, the work is already there: what you
  concluded, why you passed, what changed since.
- **Pull-line:** The first brief saves hours. The record saves them every time the company comes
  back.
- **Visual:** the record timeline graphic (spec: `04` §6.3).
- **CTA repeat** after this section: `Bring one live deal →`

## S8 · Trust & governance

- **H2:** Built for work that gets examined.
- **Commitments (set as a plain list, no icons, no badges):**
  - Your workspace is isolated. Your data serves your firm — never other customers, never model
    training.
  - AI output is a draft until a named person approves it.
  - Every record change has a documented review trail.
  - Role-based access. Export and deletion on request.
  - Source gaps are flagged, not papered over — the system says what it doesn't know.
- **Regulatory line (sober, one sentence):** Regulators are moving the same direction: documented
  human oversight of AI outputs is now an SEC examination focus for registered advisers.
- **Honesty line:** SOC 2 in progress. Security documentation available on request.

## S9 · How an engagement works (+ pricing shape)

- **H2:** Start with one workflow. Prove it on a live deal.
- **Step 1 — Workflow diagnosis.** Free, 30 minutes. Pick the workflow that hurts; map your
  sources and output format.
- **Step 2 — Implementation pilot.** Fixed fee, 30–90 days. We install MindLab, calibrate it to
  your templates, and run live deals through it with you.
- **Step 3 — Monthly system.** Keep it running and improving. Capacity-based plans from
  $2,000/month. No per-seat games.
- **Footnote-style line:** Plans set the support and usage capacity — not an artificial count of
  workflows.

## S10 · Fit

- **H2:** Built for lean deal teams.
- **Fits:** lower-mid-market PE and growth equity · independent sponsors · family offices doing
  direct deals · private credit.
- **Not a fit (yet):** firms wanting a fully autonomous pipeline · day-1 enterprise checklists
  (SSO, data residency — on the roadmap, and we'll say so honestly) · teams that want a chatbot.

*Design intent:* the "not a fit" list is a deliberate credibility device — give it equal
typographic dignity, not apologetic small print.

## S11 · Close + FAQ

- **Close H2:** Bring one live deal. Leave with a source-backed brief your team can judge.
- **CTA:** `Bring one live deal →` (opens short form: firm, role, the workflow that hurts →
  calendar)
- **FAQ (accordion or plain list; answers are 2–4 sentences each, draft below):**
  1. *How is this different from ChatGPT?* — ChatGPT answers prompts; answers die in a chat
     history. MindLab is an installed workflow: drafts are structured to your templates, carry
     sources, pass through review, and become firm records you can reuse.
  2. *How is this different from AlphaSense or Hebbia?* — Those platforms sell content breadth
     and automation. MindLab is built around what happens between AI output and your firm's
     record: the approval gate, the audit trail, and compounding memory of your own work.
  3. *Is our data used to train models?* — No. Your workspace is isolated and your data serves
     only your firm.
  4. *Where does our data live?* — In your private workspace; export and deletion on request.
     Deployment specifics are covered in the security documentation.
  5. *What exactly happens in the pilot?* — We map one workflow, configure your templates, run
     live deals through it with your team for 30–90 days, then you decide whether it becomes
     your system.
  6. *Can it match our memo format?* — Yes — that calibration is most of what the pilot is.
  7. *What if we leave?* — You export everything. The records are yours.
- **Footer:** logo mark + wordmark · the mantra, small · minimal links (Security · FAQ ·
  Contact) · tertiary capture: "One page a month on governed AI for deal teams" (email field,
  quiet) · legal line.
