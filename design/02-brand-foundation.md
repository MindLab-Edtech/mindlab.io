# 02 · Brand Foundation

## Positioning (the compass — internal, not page copy)

For lean PE deal teams who already use AI but can't put unverified output in front of an
investment committee, MindLab is **the human-approved research system for private equity**: it
turns fragmented deal and portfolio information into source-backed drafts a named human approves,
so the firm moves faster *and* keeps a defensible, compounding record. Unlike AlphaSense (content
breadth) or Hebbia (automation breadth), MindLab is built around the approval gate and the firm's
own memory.

**Category words we repeat until they're ours:** research *system* (never "tool"), approval gate,
firm record, source-backed, institutional memory.

**Fights we refuse:** content breadth (we don't sell 10,000 sources), full autonomy (the human
signature IS the product), price-led "AI for cheap".

## The mechanism the brand is built on: R3

**Resources → Reports → Records.**
Resources: the raw inputs (decks, data rooms, filings, spreadsheets, notes) — immutable.
Reports: AI-drafted, source-backed, *editable* outputs — always drafts until reviewed.
Records: firm truth — updated **only** by a human applying an approved report. Every approval
logged. AI never silently writes to the record.

Everything the brand says and shows should rhyme with this gate.

## Brand personality

**Expert. Precise. Unflashy.**

The voice of a senior colleague who has done the work: evidence-led, composed, never breathless.
Earned confidence, calm under load. A professional instrument, not a consumer app. When it makes
a claim, it shows its sources — the brand practices the product's own discipline.

## Voice rules with examples

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Every claim, one click from its source." | "Trustworthy AI you'll love!" |
| "Nothing becomes a record until someone puts their name on it." | "Fully automated end-to-end intelligence" |
| "A draft you can defend in front of the IC." | "10x your deal team" |
| "SOC 2 in progress; security documentation on request." | SOC 2 badge before certification |
| "From data room to IC-ready first pass." | "AI magic for dealmakers ✨" |

- No exclamation marks, no emoji, no rhetorical questions in headers.
- Sentences a partner could have written. Short declaratives beat clever.
- Numbers are precise and attributed, or absent.
- We name what we don't do (see "not for" section) — honesty is a brand device here.

## The three readers (design personas)

1. **Partner / Managing Director** — economic buyer. Reads: hero, stat bar, maybe the R3
   diagram. On mobile, from a forwarded link. Needs: instant credibility signals (typography,
   restraint, governance language). Allergic to: anything that looks like the AI slop their
   inbox is full of.
2. **VP / Principal** — the champion who evaluates. Reads: workflows, engagement model, fit.
   Needs: their vocabulary (CIM, data room, IC memo, covenant, watch list), a bounded low-risk
   next step, material they can forward internally.
3. **Analyst / Associate** — daily user. Reads: everything, especially product vignettes.
   Needs: to see they remain the author — review, edit, approve, sign. The product kills the
   blank page and the re-work, not their job.
4. *(Silent)* **Compliance / Ops** — checks the trust section and FAQ. Needs: isolation,
   no-training-on-customer-data, audit trail, export/deletion, stated plainly without badges.

## Anti-references (what this brand is actively against)

- **Generic AI-SaaS slop:** gradient hero, three emoji feature cards, rounded-everything,
  sparkle icons, purple-blue palettes. The whole reason this brand exists visually is to not be
  this.
- **Sparse minimalist SaaS:** a near-empty hero with one floating sentence reads as a regression
  to this audience — density is a feature, earned through hierarchy.
- **Consumer-fintech playfulness:** confetti, cartoon illustration, pastel gradients, gamified
  anything.
- **Bloomberg-terminal hostility:** dense *and* punishing. We are dense *and* legible.

forming an abstract "M" — reads as nodes/molecule/network, which happens to rhyme with

flag it with a recommendation — don't redesign unprompted.

## Fit / not-for (brand honesty, appears on page)

**For:** lower-mid-market PE and growth equity, independent sponsors, family offices doing
direct deals, private credit — lean teams where every analyst hour matters.
**Not for (yet):** firms wanting a fully autonomous pipeline; day-1 enterprise checklists
(SSO, data residency — roadmap honest); teams that just want a chatbot.
