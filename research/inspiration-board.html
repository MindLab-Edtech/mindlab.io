MindLab landing page: what “top tier” actually looks like

Captured live 2026-07-05: the category leaders (AI for elite finance & legal), the product-craft tier, and the idea galleries (Dribbble, Godly, Awwwards, SaaSLandingPage). Every screenshot below is the real page, not a mockup.

1 · The category’s top tier · AI for elite professionals

These are the pages MindLab’s exact buyer already sees. They share one language: dark cinematic ground, monumental serif in warm ivory, one demo CTA, heavyweight logo bar. They sell membership in an elite club, not features.

Harvey harvey.ai · AI for law firms

Film still of a professional marking a document. “Practice Made Perfect” in giant white serif. One CTA. Their design blog: “polish is synonymous with credibility.”

Hebbia hebbia.ai · direct competitor

“INSTITUTIONAL INTELLIGENCE”: monumental all-caps serif in cream on near-black, bronze architectural render sweeping diagonally. Logo wall: KKR, Morgan Stanley, MetLife.

Rogo rogo.ai · direct competitor

Dusk film of Manhattan + flag. “For the most ambitious firms in finance.” Serif, single demo CTA, Tiger Global / Moelis / Rothschild logo marquee. Pure prestige cinema.

Mercury mercury.com · fintech benchmark

Surreal cinematic photography (a desk on a mountain ridge), giant light sans, one action. Finance software presented as an object of desire.

Hebbia · the full playbook (scroll)

Dark monument → logo wall → product sections with real dark UI → cream stat band ($30T / 200K / 1.5B) → executive testimonial with photo → tabbed “how institutions use it” → security badge grid → cinematic close. Note the rhythm: one light band inside a dark page.

5 · Three directions for MindLab

Recommended

A · Midnight Institutional

Hebbia/Harvey’s gravity, differentiated by subject and palette: near-black page, monumental ivory serif, ledger-green + gilt accents (vs their bronze/purple), generative monumental art, dark product UI as proof, one cream stat interlude.

  • H1 candidates: “The firm that never forgets.” · “Institutional memory, installed.” · “Built for work that gets examined.”
  • Risk: same lane as competitors → we out-craft on the approval-gate moment they don’t own.

Alternative

B · Product Cinema (Linear lane)

Dark, giant clean sans, the real approvals screen as the entire hero monument. Most honest; less “private-bank prestige”, more “serious modern software”.

Alternative

C · Light Precision (Attio lane)

Light ground, huge bold sans, tabbed live product panels. Cleanest and safest; least differentiated from generic top-SaaS, least cinematic for this buyer.

The verdict in one line:

MindLab’s buyer already lives on Harvey/Hebbia/Rogo pages, where “top tier” means dark institutional cinema with monumental type and one demo CTA. MindLab should speak that language fluently, then win on the one scene competitors don’t have: a named human signing the record.
